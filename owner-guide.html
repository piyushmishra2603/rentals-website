Tenant Screening & Verification
How to find reliable, long-term tenants and avoid bad actors

SmartRent AI's KYC system pre-verifies all tenants before they can contact owners. However, you should conduct your own due diligence before signing an agreement. A 15-minute screening saves months of headaches.

Documents to Collect from Tenants
✓
Aadhaar Card — Government photo ID. Verify it is original and matches the applicant's face
✓
PAN Card — Required for agreements above ₹50,000/year for TDS compliance
✓
Employment / Offer Letter — Confirms stable income and employment status
✓
Last 3 Months' Bank Statements — Verify income regularity and financial stability
✓
Previous Landlord Reference — Strongest indicator of future behaviour as tenant
✓

⚠

Red Flags — When to Decline
✗
Applicant is unwilling to provide Aadhaar or PAN Card
✗
Wants possession immediately without prior notice to previous landlord
✗
Offers to pay rent in cash and requests no written agreement
✗
Previous landlord reference is unavailable or gives vague responses
✗

✗
Inconsistent story about reason for moving or employment status
🚫
Discrimination Warning
You may not legally refuse a tenant based on religion, caste, gender, diet, state of origin, or marital status. Doing so violates Article 15 of the Indian Constitution and can result in legal action. Your screening must be based solely on financial and character criteria.
Chapter 05
Rental Agreements — The Complete Guide
Protect yourself legally with a watertight rental agreement

A rental agreement is your most important legal protection. Never allow a tenant to move in without a signed, registered, or notarised agreement. In India, agreements of 11 months are commonly used to avoid mandatory registration, but a 12-month+ agreement requires registration under the Registration Act, 1908.

Types of Rental Agreements
📋
Leave & Licence (L&L)
Most common in Maharashtra. Licensor retains ownership at all times. Licensee has no tenancy rights. Easy to terminate. Highly recommended.
📄
Rental Agreement
Used across most states. Governed by state Rent Control Acts. If registered for 12+ months, tenant may claim stronger protection. Use 11-month agreements.
🤝
PG Agreement
Simpler format for PGs. Month-to-month, with 30-day notice clause. Specifies house rules, meals, curfew, visitor policy explicitly.
🏢
Commercial Lease
For office/retail/warehouse. Typically 3–5 years with lock-in. Include escalation, CAM charges, and fit-out approval clauses.
Mandatory Clauses in Every Agreement
✓
Full names, addresses, and Aadhaar/PAN numbers of both parties
✓
Complete property address and description (floor, area, parking)
✓
Monthly rent amount, due date, grace period (usually 5 days), and late fee
✓
Security deposit amount and conditions for deduction
✓
Lock-in period, notice period (typically 30–60 days), and termination conditions
✓
Annual rent escalation clause (e.g., 5% or 10% after 11 months)
✓
Permitted use of property (residential only / business prohibited)
✓
Utility meter numbers and responsibility for payments
✓
Maintenance responsibility matrix (who pays for what)
✓
House rules for PG: meal timings, curfew, guests, smoking, alcohol
✓
Sub-letting prohibition clause
✓
Property condition inventory (with photos attached as annexure)
⚖️
Model Tenancy Act, 2021
India's Model Tenancy Act was notified in 2021 to modernise tenancy laws. It mandates written agreements, caps deposits, and creates a dedicated Rent Authority for dispute resolution. Several states including Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, and Uttarakhand have adopted it. Check if your state has implemented it — it significantly strengthens owner rights.

Chapter 07
Legal Obligations & Owner Rights
Know your rights, duties and the laws that govern renting in India
Owner's Legal Obligations
✓
Police Verification: File police verification for every tenant within 24 hours of move-in. This is mandatory across most Indian states and protects you legally
✓
Provide Habitable Property: Property must have functional water supply, electricity, and sanitation. You cannot rent a property that fails basic habitability standards
✓
Receipt for Rent & Deposit: Always provide written receipts. This is legally required and helps both parties in dispute resolution
✓
Return Deposit: Security deposit must be returned within 30 days of vacating, after deducting documented, itemised damages. Unjustified deductions are legally actionable
✓
Notice for Entry: You must give 24–48 hours' advance notice before entering the tenant's premises. Unannounced entry violates the tenant's right to quiet enjoyment
✓
Proper Notice to Vacate: Follow the notice period stipulated in the agreement (usually 30–60 days). You cannot evict without due process
Eviction — The Right Way

Eviction in India follows a specific legal process. Self-help eviction (changing locks, cutting utilities, removing belongings) is illegal and can result in criminal charges against you. The correct process involves:

1
Issue Legal Notice
Send a written legal notice via registered post citing the specific breach (non-payment, agreement violation, etc.) with a 15–30 day cure period.
2
File Before Rent Authority / Civil Court
If the tenant fails to comply, file an eviction petition before the local Rent Authority (under Model Tenancy Act states) or Civil Court. Attach agreement, notice, and evidence.
3
Court Order & Execution
A court order for possession will be issued if your case is valid. Execution by a court bailiff follows. This process takes 6–24 months — hence the importance of thorough tenant screening.
⚖️
Key Laws Every Owner Must Know
Transfer of Property Act, 1882 — Governs lease agreements and tenant rights
Registration Act, 1908 — Mandatory registration for leases exceeding 12 months
Model Tenancy Act, 2021 — Most progressive national framework
Rent Control Acts — State-specific (Maharashtra Rent Control Act, Karnataka Rent Act, etc.)
Income Tax Act, 1961 — Sections 22–27 cover house property income
Chapter 08
Tax on Rental Income — Owner's Guide
Understand your tax obligations and maximise legitimate deductions

Rental income is taxable in India under the head "Income from House Property" (Section 22 of IT Act). However, the tax framework allows significant deductions that can substantially reduce your tax liability.

How Rental Income is Taxed
ComponentTreatment
Annual Rental Value (ARV)Base amount — all rent received/receivable in a year
Municipal Tax PaidDeductible: Actual amount paid to municipal authority
Net Annual Value (NAV)ARV minus Municipal Tax = NAV
Standard Deduction (Section 24a)30% of NAV — allowed without proof
Home Loan Interest (Section 24b)Up to ₹2 lakh per year for self-occupied + unlimited for let-out
Home Loan Principal (Section 80C)Up to ₹1.5 lakh per year
TDS by TenantIf annual rent > ₹2.4 lakh, tenant must deduct 10% TDS and deposit via Form 26QC
💰
Example Tax Calculation (FY 2025–26)
Annual Rent: ₹12,00,000 | Municipal Tax: ₹24,000
NAV = ₹12,00,000 − ₹24,000 = ₹11,76,000
Standard Deduction (30%) = ₹3,52,800
Home Loan Interest = ₹2,00,000
Taxable Income = ₹11,76,000 − ₹3,52,800 − ₹2,00,000 = ₹6,23,200
At 30% slab = ₹1,86,960 tax (without other exemptions)
💡
If you own 2+ let-out properties, you can set off their losses against each other
💡
GST of 18% applies to commercial property rent exceeding ₹20 lakh/year turnover — register for GST
💡
Maintain a dedicated bank account for rental income — makes ITR filing and audit much simpler
⚠
Cash rent above ₹2 lakh in a single transaction is prohibited under Section 269ST — always use bank transfer
⚠
Failure to report rental income is treated as tax evasion — penalties of 200% of tax owed can apply
Chapter 09
Frequently Asked Owner Questions
Answers to the most common questions from property owners
Can I evict a tenant for not paying rent? ▼
Yes, but only through the legal process. Issue a written notice (15–30 days), then file an eviction petition before the Rent Authority or Civil Court. Do NOT lock them out, cut utilities, or remove their belongings — this is illegal and can result in criminal charges against you, regardless of non-payment.
How do I handle a tenant who refuses to vacate after notice? ▼
File an eviction suit before the District Court or Rent Authority. Attach the agreement, termination notice (sent via registered post), and any evidence of breach. In Model Tenancy Act states, the Rent Authority can expedite matters. Avoid self-help eviction at all costs.
Do I need to register my rental agreement? ▼
Agreements for less than 12 months do not require mandatory registration (though they can be notarised for ₹200–500). Agreements for 12 months or more must be registered with the Sub-Registrar's Office under Section 17 of the Registration Act. Unregistered 12-month+ agreements are inadmissible as evidence in court.
My society is refusing to allow PG tenants — what can I do? ▼
Housing societies can impose restrictions on PGs through their registered bye-laws. First, review the society's registered bye-laws. If they permit PGs with conditions (police verification, approval), comply with those. If the restriction is arbitrary and not part of registered bye-laws, you can challenge it before the Registrar of Co-operative Societies or approach a consumer forum.
What deductions can I make from the security deposit? ▼
You may deduct for: unpaid rent, documented property damage beyond normal wear and tear (scratches, broken fixtures, stained walls), unpaid utility bills, and professional cleaning costs if the property was left in an unusable state. You cannot deduct for: normal wear and tear, pre-existing damage (documented at move-in), or vague "miscellaneous" charges. Provide itemised receipts for every deduction.
Is TDS applicable to my rental income? ▼
If your annual rent exceeds ₹2,40,000 from a single tenant who is an individual/HUF, the tenant must deduct TDS at 10% under Section 194IB and deposit it with Form 26QC. If the tenant doesn't deduct TDS, you must still declare the full rent in your ITR. For commercial properties, TDS at 10% applies under Section 194I when annual rent exceeds ₹2,40,000.
Can I run a PG from a property that has a home loan? ▼
Most home loan agreements include a clause requiring the lender's consent for subletting or commercial use. Check your loan agreement carefully. In practice, most banks do not object to residential renting, but running a formal PG business may require their NOC. Violating the terms can result in the loan being called in (full repayment demanded). Always seek written permission when in doubt.
How do I screen tenants from other states or cities? ▼
Use SmartRent AI's verified profile system — all tenants on our platform have submitted Aadhaar verification. Additionally, you can conduct video calls to verify identity, request employment letters from HR on company letterhead, and ask for 3 months' bank statements via secure upload. Our platform also shows a trust score for each tenant based on their verification level and platform history.
